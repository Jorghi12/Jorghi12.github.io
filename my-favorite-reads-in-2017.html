Books · 2017

Favorite Books of 2017

By Jörg Doku · Published July 12, 2020 · 2017 reading notes

Over the course of the year I read broadly across business, careers, power, and long-range change. These were a few of the books that expanded my thinking the most.

Top picks

The Hard Thing About Hard Things

Ben Horowitz

For an unvarnished look at the challenges of starting a company, Ben Horowitz delivers. He covers crucial topics like hiring for different company stages and the difficult task of firing personnel.

The Everything Store

Brad Stone

This well-crafted narrative captures the essence of both Jeff Bezos and Amazon, highlighting their distinctive qualities and impact on the business world.

Principles

Ray Dalio

Dalio makes a compelling case for the importance of well-thought-out principles in leading a fulfilling life.

The Startup of You

Reid Hoffman

Hoffman reframes the concept of a startup, focusing on personal career development and how to build a career with real leverage.

The Black Swan

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

Taleb introduces the idea of rare, hard-to-predict events with outsized consequences, and how easily people rationalize them after the fact.

The Future of Professions

Daniel Susskind and Richard Susskind

The Susskinds predict a radical shift in society and emphasize the need to prepare for deep structural changes in how expertise is delivered.

In summary

These books expanded how I thought about company building, careers, and structural change. They still read like a snapshot of first principles and long-range thinking.
