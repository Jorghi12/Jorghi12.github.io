Books · 2019

Favorite Books of 2019

This year I expanded the breadth of what I normally read and found a handful of books that significantly changed how I think.

Top picks

The Beginning of Infinity

David Deutsch

An exploration of the nature of knowledge and progress.

The Incerto

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

A collection including Fooled by Randomness, The Black Swan, The Bed of Procrustes, Antifragile, and Skin in the Game, exploring uncertainty and randomness.

The Innovator's Dilemma

Clayton Christensen

A seminal work on disruptive innovation in business.

The Structure of Scientific Revolutions

Thomas S. Kuhn

A landmark study in the history and philosophy of science.

Thinking, Fast and Slow

Daniel Kahneman

An in-depth exploration of the two systems that drive the way we think.

In summary

These books shaped how I think about knowledge, uncertainty, business, and decision-making. They still hold up as a compact reading list for changing how you reason.
